LABRADOR PARK

A Place like No Other!
Find joy in nature.

By Allysha Lim.

Introduction

Located in the South of Singapore with its own dedicated MRT Station on the Circle Line, Labrador Park is a park that holds much of Singapore’s history amongst the flora and fauna. It is the only rocky sea-cliff area on the Singapore mainland that is accessible to public.

At Labrador Park, there are many activities to enjoy, like fishing, barbecuing and jogging, but many would agree that the main attraction is the scenery.


History

There is a symbolic replica of Long Ya Men located at Labrador Park. When Chinese explorer Wang Dayuan came to Singaproe for the first time via Keppel Harbour, there were rocky outcrops that looked similar to dragon teeth in Wang's eyes. Therefore the rocky outcrop was named Dragon's Teeth Gate or Long Ya Men.The original Long Ya Men was destroyed by the British in 1848 to make way for larger ships, but in 2005, this replica was erected by the government near the original site of Long Ya Men.

Before the land was reclaimed, the area was formerly known as Pasir Panjang Beach. Pasir Panjang Beach was home to a British military base called Fort Pasir Panjang.When the British surrendered to the Japanese in 1942, during World War 2, the fort becamed abandoned. As the site was developed into a nature reserve and park, many of the original military tunnels and falcilities still remain. Some are even on display.


Wildlife

Labrador Park is actually officially known as Labrador Nature Reserve. It is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. Sometimes, you may even see wild chickens with their chicks or even wild peacocks on a leisurely stroll.
